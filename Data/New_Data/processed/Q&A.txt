How do I apply for admission? What documents are needed?
Admission to JKKNIU is conducted through the GST-integrated public university entrance test. Applicants must apply online via the GST admission portal at gstadmission.ac.bd. During the application process, you need to upload scanned copies of your SSC and HSC transcripts, a passport-size photograph, and the payment receipt as proof of application fee payment.

Where do I check exam schedules, admit cards, and eligibility?
All official notices, exam schedules, admit cards, and seat plans are published on the GST admission portal and JKKNIU's official website. Candidates should regularly check these portals to stay updated on exam-related information.

Can I transfer credits from another institution?
There is no publicly available information on credit transfer policies at JKKNIU. Generally, credit transfer requires a formal petition to the Academic Council, and you should confirm the details and procedure with the Registrar's office.

Can I change my department or pursue a dual major?
Changing departments or opting for dual majors is generally not permitted once the program has begun. Exceptions may be made if special permission is granted after admission, but these are rare and subject to university regulations.

Is JKKNIU's academic system open-credit or fixed-credit?
JKKNIU follows a fixed credit system where students must complete prescribed courses as per the curriculum. Flexibility in credit selection is limited compared to open-credit systems.

How qualified are the teachers at JKKNIU?
The university employs over 160 full-time faculty members, including Professors, Associate Professors, Assistant Professors, and Lecturers, ensuring a strong academic environment.

What are the best departments at JKKNIU, and what is the status of the CSE department?
According to EduRank, JKKNIU is recognized for its publications in Computer Science and Environmental Science. The Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) was one of the first four established departments and benefits from modern academic and laboratory facilities.

How many faculties and departments does JKKNIU have, and what are the GPA criteria for admission?
JKKNIU has six faculties encompassing 25 departments across Science & Engineering, Arts, Business, Social Sciences, Fine Arts, and Law. Minimum GPA requirements for admission vary by discipline; applicants should refer to the annual admission circular available on the GST portal.

Is there an admission test, and where can past questions be found?
Yes, an admission test is required for all units under the GST system. Past questions may be accessed through senior student groups on social media platforms like Facebook or via preparatory coaching centers.

Faculties & Research

How many faculties and departments are there?
JKKNIU consists of six faculties and twenty-five departments, all operational and offering various academic programs.

Library & IT Support

What library services does JKKNIU provide?
The library offers automated services for acquisition, circulation, and reference management through Koha and DSpace digital repositories. It houses over 32,000 books, subscribes to 53 journals, and provides cyber facilities for users.

What digital support is available for students and faculty?
Free access to computing resources and internet is provided across campus. Facilities for photocopying and scanning are also available.

Campus Life, Events & Administration

What residential halls and sports facilities are available?
The campus offers four residential halls named Agnibeena, Doloncapa, Bidrohi, and Shiulimala. There are sports grounds where students regularly participate in inter-university competitions.

What security and student support services are provided?
Located on a rural campus of approximately 57 acres, JKKNIU maintains standard security measures including proctors and university help-lines to ensure student safety and support.
