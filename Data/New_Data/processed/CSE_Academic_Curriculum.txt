Credit Distribution Summary - CSE at JKKNIU
The B.Sc. (Engg.) degree in Computer Science and Engineering requires the successful completion of 165.00 total credits. The majority of the curriculum is dedicated to core CSE (Departmental) courses, which account for 118.50 credits (71.81%). The remaining credits are distributed among foundational and interdisciplinary subjects: Mathematics and General Education (GED) each contribute 15.0 credits (9.10%), followed by Electrical and Electronic Engineering (EEE) with 13.50 credits (8.18%), and Physics making up the final 3.00 credits (1.81%) of the program requirements.

Program Duration and Grading System - CSE at JKKNIU
The B.Sc. (Engg.) program in Computer Science and Engineering is a four-year degree divided into eight semesters, with a total requirement of 165 credit hours. Each academic year consists of two regular semesters (Semester I and Semester II), and the entire program must be completed within a maximum of six consecutive academic years. A standard semester spans 19 weeks, which includes 14 weeks for classes, a 2-week recess before final examinations, and 3 weeks for the semester final examinations. Credits are assigned based on the workload: one hour of theoretical lecture per week is equivalent to 1.0 credit (14 total lecture hours), while one laboratory session of two hours per week is equivalent to 1.0 credit (28 total lab hours).
Distribution of Marks and Evaluation
The marking system distinguishes between theory, laboratory, and project-based courses. For Theory Courses, the total 100% marks are divided into Continuous Assessment (40%)—comprising Class Attendance (10%) and Midterms/Assignments (30%)—and the Semester Final Examination (60%). Laboratory Courses also follow a 40/60 split, where Continuous Assessment (40%) includes attendance (10%), lab reports (10%), and continuous evaluation (20%), while the Lab Final (60%) includes a viva-voce (20%), lab test (30%), and answer script (10%). For the Project and Thesis, the supervisor provides a 40% continuous assessment, and the remaining 60% is determined during the final examination through a report (10%), demonstration (30%), and presentation/viva-voce (20%). Class attendance marks are tiered, awarding the full 10% for attendance of 90% or above, and decreasing to 0% if attendance falls below 60%.

GPA and CGPA Calculation - CSE at JKKNIU
Student performance is measured using a letter grade system ranging from A+ (4.00, Outstanding) for marks of 80% and above, down to D (2.00, Poor) for marks between 40% and 45%. Any score less than 40% is graded as F (0.00, Fail). The Grade Point Average (GPA) is calculated as the weighted average of the grade points obtained in all courses in a semester using the formula: GPA = (Sum of [Course Credits × Grade Points]) / (Total Credits in Semester). The Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) follows a similar weighted average across all completed semesters: CGPA = (Sum of [Total Semester Credits × Semester GPA]) / (Total Credits completed).

Promotion Requirements - CSE at JKKNIU
To be promoted to the next academic year, students must meet specific CGPA thresholds: 2.00 to move from 1st to 2nd year, 2.25 for 2nd to 3rd year, and 2.50 for 3rd to 4th year. To be awarded the Bachelor of Engineering degree, a student must complete a minimum of 160 credits and secure a minimum CGPA of 2.5. Exceptional students who achieve an average GPA of 3.80 or above in two regular semesters of an academic year are recognized on the Dean's List, provided they have no "F" grades that year. Students with a CGPA of 3.80 or above upon graduation receive their degree with Distinction, provided no "F" grades were earned during the entire program.
Improvement of Grades and Regulations
Students obtaining a grade of B or below (3.00 or less) are permitted to improve their grades by retaking the examination with the next immediate batch. However, students can improve a maximum of two courses per year (6 credit hours) and no more than 8 courses total throughout the entire program. If a student fails to earn the required yearly GPA for promotion, even after taking readmission, they will be dropped out of the program. Additionally, the university maintains a strict policy against unfair means, and no credit transfers from other programs or institutions are allowed.
