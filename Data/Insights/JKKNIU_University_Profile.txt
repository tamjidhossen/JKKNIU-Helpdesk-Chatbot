Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University (JKKNIU) History
The historical development of Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University (JKKNIU) began with an initiative by the Executive Committee of the National Economic Commission (ECNEC) in February 2004, leading to the laying of its foundation stone by Prime Minister Khaleda Zia on March 1, 2005. The university was officially inaugurated on May 25, 2007, and while it was originally envisioned as the first culture-based university in Bangladesh, the University Act of 2006 established it as a general university with a specific emphasis on liberal arts education. Under the leadership of its first Vice-Chancellor, Mohammad Shamsur Rahman, the academic program commenced on June 3, 2007, with an initial batch of 185 students across four foundational departments: Bengali Language & Literature, English Language & Literature, Music, and Computer Science and Engineering. It is the first university established to preserve the memory of National Poet Kazi Nazrul Islam.

Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University (JKKNIU) History - Expansion
In the years following its inception, JKKNIU underwent rapid expansion. In 2008, the Faculty of Business Administration was introduced with the Department of Accounting and Information Systems, and the Faculty of Social Science was established with the Department of Economics. Expansion continued in the 2009–2010 academic year with the addition of the Department of Finance and Banking and the Department of Fine Arts. By the 2010–2011 academic year, the university further diversified its academic offerings by establishing the Departments of Dramatics, Electronics & Communication Engineering, Public Administration, and Human Resource Management across its existing faculties.

Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University (JKKNIU) Location
The campus of Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University (JKKNIU) is situated at Namapara Battala in Trishal, Mymensingh. It is strategically located approximately 22 km from the Mymensingh city center and just 3 km away from the Dhaka-Mymensingh Highway. For those traveling from the capital, the university is roughly 100 km away from Dhaka. The specific location, "Battala," is named after a legendary and historic banyan tree that has stood for many years at Namapara, serving as a local landmark for the campus.

Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University (JKKNIU) Faculties Overview
Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University (JKKNIU) operates through 25 departments organized under 6 distinct faculties, offering a diverse range of studies from technical engineering to fine arts. The Faculty of Science and Engineering houses technical disciplines including Computer Science and Engineering, Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Environmental Science & Engineering, and Statistics. The Faculty of Arts is the largest, covering Bangla and English Language and Literature, Theatre and Performance Studies, Music, Film & Media Studies, Philosophy, and History. Business education is managed by the Faculty of Business Administration, which includes Accounting and Information Systems, Finance and Banking, Human Resource Management, Management, and Marketing. Social studies are conducted under the Faculty of Social Science, comprising Economics, Public Administration & Governance Studies, Local Government and Urban Development, Folklore, Anthropology, Population Sciences, and Sociology. Specialized professional and creative education is provided by the Faculty of Fine Arts and the Faculty of Law, which hosts the Department of Law & Justice.

Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University (JKKNIU) Infrastructure - Academic Buildings
To facilitate its broad curriculum, JKKNIU utilizes six primary academic buildings. These include the Arts Building, the Old Science Building, the Social Science Building, the Business Administration Building, the New Science Building, and the New Arts Building. Each facility is equipped to handle the specific needs of its respective departments, from science laboratories to performance studios.

Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University (JKKNIU) Infrastructure - Residential Halls
The university provides residential facilities for its students through four major halls. These halls are named after iconic works or themes related to the National Poet, reflecting the university's cultural identity: Agnibeena Hall, Doloncapa Hall, Bidrohi Hall, and Shiulimala Hall. These residences serve as the primary living spaces for students coming from various parts of the country.

Institutes and Quality Assurance at JKKNIU
In addition to its core faculties, the university features the Institute of Nazrul Studies, dedicated to researching and preserving the works and legacy of the National Poet Kazi Nazrul Islam. To maintain and enhance educational standards, the university also operates an Institutional Quality Assurance Cell (IQAC), which ensures that academic and administrative processes meet rigorous quality benchmarks.

Research and Extension Center at JKKNIU
The Research and Extension Center, Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University, was established on 23 September 2013, according to the decision of the 43rd Syndicate Meeting of this university. Since its inception, the Center has been playing a major role in providing financial and logistic support for researchers to produce and disseminate knowledge among national and international academia. The Center relentlessly works on its main goal of expansion and quality development of research. This Center grants funds to nominated researchers every fiscal year following their selection through a blind peer review process. Additionally, the Research and Extension Center offers PhD fellowships to a certain number of students each year. Funding is also provided to shortlisted Master's (thesis) students to facilitate their research and improve its quality. Seminars and workshops are regularly organized with the presence of renowned researchers and scientists of the country to share research findings among the academic community. The Center is led by a Director appointed by the university authority, who also acts as the member secretary of the Institutional Review Board (IRB) of JKKNIU. Currently, Dr. Md. Habibur Rahman, Associate Professor, Department of Finance and Banking, serves as Director.

Cultural Life and Campus Traditions at JKKNIU
Cultural activities are central to the university's identity, reflecting its namesake, National Poet Kazi Nazrul Islam. The most significant event is the annual celebration of Nazrul’s birthday. Another unique tradition is the two-day Kuyasha Utshab (Winter Festival), which features photography and painting exhibitions, traditional stalls, film screenings, and musical performances. The campus also hosts annual celebrations for Rabindranath Tagore's birthday. Additionally, Radio JKKNIU serves as a student-run radio station, providing a platform for student voices and creative content.

Notable Research Contributions at JKKNIU
The university is actively involved in significant scientific research. Most notably, Ashraf Ali Seddique, the head of the Department of Environmental Science and Engineering, led a research team that discovered high levels of uranium in Cox's Bazar. This finding stands as a major contribution to environmental science and resource exploration in Bangladesh.

Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University (JKKNIU) Distinguished Faculty and Alumni
JKKNIU is home to many prominent figures in literature, research, and the arts. Notable faculty members include Jatin Sarkar (writer and thinker), Khondakar Ashraf Hossain (poet, translator, and 3rd VC), Mohit Ul Alam (Shakespearian scholar and 4th VC), and Soumitra Sekhar Dey (noted researcher and 6th VC).
